A FOUR - DIMENSIONAL CROSSWORD PUZZLE

The usual directions "Across" and "Down" operate within the individual pieces of grid. The "Hyper-" directions jump from a spot in one grid to a corresponding spot in the next grid across or down. To help illustrate this, the spot (19 Hyper-Down) has been highlighted. The grid can be considered a subset of a 5x5x5x5 hyper-cube, although trying to visualise this in four dimensions may not be any help in solving the puzzle.

There are exactly four cells of the grid which are maximally cross-checked, being part of spots in all four directions. These special points therefore each have five clues relating to them, counting this one (which may have been news to you).

The Hyper-spots are all five cells long, so I haven't indicated the length with each clue.
